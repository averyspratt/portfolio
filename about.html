I like to call myself a
MAKER

I'm currently a senior at Tufts University, majoring in Engineering Psychology and minoring in Computer Science and Studio Art.

Human Factors

The human-centered approach is at the heart of all that I do. My time at Tufts and my diverse job experience have trained me in research, problem solving, and implementation of solutions derived from the root of all great design - the user.
Be it for physical or digital systems, my expertise in human factors drives my design process to create accessible and enjoyable user experiences, built from intentional research and effective collaboration.

Fine Art

I love getting lost in the intimate technical details of my work (hence the concentration in highly technical media like printmaking and embroidery)
My experience with fine arts and the creative process greatly influences my work in any field. Beyond strengthening my eye for good design, my art is another tool for communication and, lets face it, good communication drives good design.

Computer Science

I got hooked with computer science during the introductory course at Tufts. The element of computer science I connect with the most is the problem solving. Projects from coding a snake game in C++ or developing this website, energize me in that super rewarding Type-II fun kind of way. In my user experience work, my knowledge of computer science allows me to more successfully collaborate with developers.

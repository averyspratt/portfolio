The Laws of UX

January 30, 2018

The Initial User Research phase of development is quickly coming to an end. From here, I plan to develop initial concept prototypes for further testing. These concepts will be designed based on the findings of the initial research. As I sort through takeaways from the literature review, suggestions from expert interviews, and everything in between, there is a lot of data to help inform and inspire my designs. However, at a stage like this, I think it is also important to refresh on the fundamental principles of interface design. Earlier today, I stumbled upon this inspirational site:
The Laws of UX
10 UX principles and maxims packaged in a wonderfully designed site - what more could a UXer ask for?! I am positive this will remain in browser tab bar as I develop the concept prototypes. One concept that I found particularly interesting, especially within the context of this project is the Zeigarnik Effect.

"People remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed tasks."

How might this apply to my product, especially with such a young target user group? How might I track students progress in a way that encourages future (and frequent) engagement? These questions (and many more) are ones I am asking myself as I enter the next stage of product development.
Excited for more to come!
-Avery
